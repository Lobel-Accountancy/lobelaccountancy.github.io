Background

Career & Credentials

Jeffrey began his career in public accounting at Baker Tilly, one of the nation's top advisory and assurance firms, where he rose from staff to senior roles serving clients across California. His engagements at Baker Tilly spanned the full assurance spectrum — financial statement audits for large public companies with SOX internal control requirements, an IPO transaction, and a full range of mid-market audits, reviews, and compilations for privately held businesses across industries including specialty finance, manufacturing, real estate, and professional services. That grounding gave him something most controllers never acquire: a deep command of GAAS audit methodology built from the auditor's side of the table, not learned from managing one.

He later moved into industry as Controller at a California-based specialty finance company — a role that expanded his scope considerably. There, he was responsible for all external financial reporting across portfolios totaling more than $600 million in managed receivables. That meant leading multi-entity consolidated audits with national CPA firms, managing the full debt covenant compliance cycle, building automated accounting infrastructure from the ground up, and presenting board-level financial packages to investors and lenders. On the technical side, the work covered CECL adoption under ASC 326, securitization accounting under ASC 860, multi-entity consolidation, and lender reporting across multiple credit facilities — the kind of breadth most accountants encounter in pieces, not all at once.

Jeffrey holds a degree from the University of Oregon's Charles H. Lundquist College of Business and is actively licensed as a CPA in California. His Certified Fraud Examiner (CFE) credential — awarded by the Association of Certified Fraud Examiners — adds a trained eye on internal controls, risk environments, and financial integrity that goes beyond what most CPAs bring to an engagement.

At LAC, Jeffrey works directly on every client engagement. There are no handoffs to junior staff — the senior expertise you engage is the expertise you receive.

Technical Capabilities

Your engagement moves faster because the person running it can also fix the systems causing the delays.

Most CPAs are highly skilled at accounting. Fewer can also rebuild the infrastructure that accounting runs on. Jeffrey's technical fluency in automation, data transformation, and workbook engineering means LAC engagements don't just produce deliverables — they leave your financial operations in better shape than they were.

Power Query Automation
Rebuilding manual, error-prone consolidation workbooks into automated pipelines. Multi-source data transformation, scheduled refresh logic, and elimination of copy-paste close processes that introduce risk.
Advanced Excel & Macro Engineering
Complex Excel models — not just formulas. VBA macros for automated report generation, workbook restructuring, and close-cycle task automation. Replacing hours of manual work with reliable, documented processes.
Python for Financial Consolidation
Custom Python scripts for consolidating large numbers of subsidiary workbooks, normalizing chart of accounts across entities, and building year-end reporting packages that would otherwise take weeks of manual effort.
Workbook Architecture & Restructuring
Redesigning accounting workbooks from the ground up — clear separation of inputs, calculations, and outputs; version-controlled structures; and documentation that survives staff turnover.
Month-End & Audit Schedule Optimization
Mapping the close cycle from end to end, identifying bottlenecks, and rebuilding the sequence and ownership of tasks. Clients who go through this process typically reduce close time by 30–60%.
PBC Package & Audit Infrastructure
Building the prepared-by-client documentation that makes audits efficient. Organized, cross-referenced, clearly labeled support packages that reduce auditor questions and compress fieldwork timelines.

The goal is never automation for its own sake. It's accuracy, speed, and resilience — financial infrastructure that holds up under scrutiny, scales with the business, and doesn't break when someone goes on vacation.
