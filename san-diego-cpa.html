Services for San Diego Businesses

Accounting & CPA Services in San Diego

San Diego's business community is diverse — technology startups, biotech and life sciences companies, government contractors, real estate operators, and mid-market manufacturers. LAC's technical range covers the accounting needs that matter most across these sectors, with particular depth in complex entity structures, technical GAAP standards, and audit engagements.

Audit, Review & Compilation
Full-scope GAAS financial statement audits, review engagements for lender and investor requirements, and compilations for internal or third-party use. Multi-entity and consolidated engagements. First-time audits managed from pre-audit preparation through fieldwork completion and final report issuance.
Financial Statement Preparation
GAAP financial statements with complete footnote disclosures. Technical standard implementation — ASC 606 for technology and SaaS companies, ASC 842 lease accounting, CECL — handled by the same CPA who drafted the technical memo. Suitable for investor review, lender distribution, or audit support.
Fractional CFO & Controller
Part-time senior financial leadership for San Diego companies preparing for fundraising, navigating a first audit, or building out financial reporting infrastructure. Run by a CPA with both public accounting and corporate controller experience — the full stack, not just one side of it.
Technical Accounting & Audit Readiness
Technical accounting memo drafting, complex standard adoption, and pre-audit preparation for San Diego businesses facing investor diligence or regulatory review. Accounting positions documented and defensible before anyone external reviews them.

Why San Diego Companies Choose LAC

Senior-level work without the large-firm overhead

San Diego businesses often face a familiar choice when they need CPA services: a large regional firm that will staff the engagement with associates and manage it from a distance, or a small local CPA who lacks the technical depth for complex work. LAC is a deliberate alternative to both.

Technical depth that matches San Diego's market: San Diego is home to highly technical businesses — life sciences companies with complex revenue recognition, defense contractors with multi-contract structures, technology startups preparing for Series A diligence. Jeffrey's background covers the technical accounting standards these companies encounter: ASC 606, complex consolidations, securitization accounting, multi-entity audit engagements.

No staffing overhead: Large firms serving San Diego bring overhead — multiple billing rates, lengthy onboarding, and a staffing model that assigns your engagement to whoever is available. LAC's model is simpler: Jeffrey Lobel works directly on your engagement. That directness compresses timelines and eliminates the translation layer between partner and associate that creates errors and delays.

Systems capability: When the accounting problem is also an infrastructure problem — a manual consolidation process, a close cycle that takes too long, a reporting package built on a foundation of copy-paste — LAC can fix it. That technical capability is what separates an engagement that produces a deliverable from one that improves the business.
